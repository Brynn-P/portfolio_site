About Me

From protecting landscapes to defending networks

My Journey

To say my path to cybersecurity is unconventional is to put it lightly. After many years working in wildlife conservation and physical security, I discovered that the principles of observation, risk assessment, and systematic thinking apply equally to protecting the technological landscape.

What began as curiosity, me sitting in the middle of the bush, learning html, css and basic JS through an android app, quickly grew into a fascination with understanding how systems operate. This evolved into a passion for understanding how they can be broken‚Äîand more importantly, how to defend them. I view the transition from field work to cybersecurity not as a pivot but rather a natural progression of the same core mission: protection.

"Security is not a product, but a process" - Bruce Schneier

Security is not just about the technology, it is about the deep understanding of systems, this understanding enables you to identify vulnerabilities, and provides you the right methods and tools to enable you to think like both defender and adversary.

My Philosophy

My approach to cybersecurity is with the mindset of a conservationist: when seeking to understand security you will find that it requires seeing the entire ecosystem, not just individual components. Every system has vulnerabilities, and at times the most effective defense is offence, this requires thinking like both protector and adversary.

My background in field work has taught me many things some of which include patience, observation, and systematic documentation‚Äîskills that carry through directly to security research. Whether tracking wildlife or tracking cyber threats, the fundamentals remain the same: understand the environment, find the patterns, and stay prepared.
