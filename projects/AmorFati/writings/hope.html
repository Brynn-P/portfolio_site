Hope.

A reflection on hope, reality, and truth.

Hope.

I want to start off by quoting what is known as the serenity prayer that has been attributed to Reinhold Niebuhr, and it goes as follows, “God, grant me the serenity to accept the things I cannot change, the courage to change the things I can, and the wisdom to know the difference.”

Hope can be an amazing thing, but hope can too be a very dangerous thing.

I have seen hope carry many people through unimaginably difficult times, but I have also seen souls destroyed because of it.

Take for example my home country, Zimbabwe, many people and to some degree myself included lived on little more than hope and dream of a better tomorrow, and yet the stark reality is hope alone has not fixed anything, after Mugabe was overthrown Zimbabweans had hope and a year later those that brought them hope gunned them down in the streets, even then there was still hope and yet they still starve.

This is not me saying one should give up entirely and resign yourself to your fate, because that too is just as fatal but rather as hard as it may be, look at the situations in your life realistically and honestly and focus on the things you can alter.

The same goes for relationships whether it is an intimate relationship, family, or even a friendship.

I myself have found myself holding onto unrealistic hopes of a change in heart from another or hoping that they may choose a different course of action and in doing so I have caused more damage to my own well-being than would have been done had I looked at the situation honestly and accepted it for what it is, and moved on and refocused my hope and energy’s into things I know I am able to effect.

Hope is a good thing, when it’s placed in the right places.

But don’t get so lost hoping for a better tomorrow that you stop trying to make one for yourself.

“Rather than love, than money, than fame, give me truth.” Henry David Thoreau
